üîê Understanding OTP (One-Time Password)

What is OTP?

OTP is a security code sent to YOUR phone when YOU initiate a transaction. It's like a temporary password that expires quickly.

Critical Facts:

  • OTP is ONLY for you - Never share it with anyone, including bank employees
  • Banks never ask for OTP - No legitimate company will call and ask for it
  • OTP = Money Transfer - Sharing OTP can allow someone to withdraw money from your account
  • Check before entering - Always read what transaction the OTP is for before entering it

üö® Remember: If someone asks for OTP, it's a SCAM. No exceptions!

üì± How to Report a Scam

Step 1: Immediate Action

  1. Stop all communication with scammer
  2. Block the phone number
  3. If you shared bank details, call your bank immediately
  4. Change all passwords

Step 2: Report to Authorities

Call National Helpline:

1930

Online Reporting:

Visit cybercrime.gov.in

Step 3: Gather Evidence

  • Screenshot all messages
  • Note phone numbers and call times
  • Save emails or SMS
  • Record transaction details
  • Note UPI IDs or account numbers
